Week 8

Sewing and Embroidery

First off, I need to desgin a file that is used to sew on a hat by the machine, Tajima Sai.

I roughly drew a picture of a cool looking face in photoshop using my wacom board :), then I set up the frame (it was pretty hard since my hat's material is too hard).

I layed two layers of backing, the first is just another plain fabric, the second is a layer wet-laid nonwoven backing.

After changing the needle and the bobbin, the machine quickly embroyed the nice rough image on my hat.


After making the hat, we went on to something more manual, sewing.

We spent one class talking about the basics and then we need to make our own pillow case using three different patterned fabric.

I chose three bright warm colored fabric for a gift for my mom. The process is pretty complicated, we first pin the two fabric together, then roll it inside out and pin the third.

We sew one straight line to connect the three fabric, then we roll the front back out.

After ironing it, we cut the extra fabric and sew the rest of the sides to seal the pillow case (except one way for the entrance)

Then we iron it again, Finally, we flip the pillow case inside out and do a French sew on the egde.

Dah Dah~ we got our custume made pillow case :)

The biggest challenge is the very first pinning part, where I need to be so careful not to pin and sew in a slightly off angle.

I learned how to control the direction of sewing and lining up the place where I need to sew
